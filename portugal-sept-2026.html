Group Notes
Before We Go

⚠️ Emily — Coconut Allergy

Flag coconut at restaurants, especially desserts and pastries. Pastéis de Nata are coconut-free ✓. Ask staff when ordering anything unfamiliar.

🥂 Emily — Non-Drinker

All selected venues have great non-alcoholic options. Portugal standard: Sumol passionfruit soda, fresh-squeezed juice, sparkling water. Nobody makes it weird.

🐟 Emily — Fish-Flexible

Emily generally avoids fish but is willing to try some — she's more flexible than a hard no. Call ahead at seafood-heavy spots to confirm alternative options. See ⚠️ flags in itinerary.

🦞 Jess + 1 Other — Seafood Lovers

Keep all seafood restaurants in the plan. Most spots have strong meat dishes alongside seafood. No mussels or clams for Jess.

Entry Requirements
Docs & Border Info

🛂 EES — Active & Affects Your Trip

Launched April 10, 2026. On arrival at FAO, all 3 travelers submit fingerprints + facial scan at passport control. Nothing to prep — happens automatically. Allow extra queue time. Check all 3 passports are valid through March 8, 2027.

✅ ETIAS — Not Required for September 2026

EU pre-travel authorization launches Q4 2026, won't be mandatory until ~April 2027. No action needed for this trip.

Visa
Not required for US passport holders (Schengen, 90 days)
Currency
Euro. Cards widely accepted. Bring cash for Casinha do Petisco + Taberna da Rua das Flores (cash only).
Tipping
Not expected. Rounding up or €1–2 appreciated.
Rideshare
Uber works in Lisbon + major Algarve towns.
