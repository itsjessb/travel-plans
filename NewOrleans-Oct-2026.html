Voodoo, Tarot & the Mystical
Jackson Square and surrounding blocks — this is your territory.
Voodoo Authentica
612 Dumaine St · French Quarter
The most legitimate voodoo shop in the city. In-house practitioners, authentic gris-gris, readings, potions. Not a gift shop. They take it seriously — you should too or don't go in.
Earth Odyssey
Near Jackson Square · earthodysseynola.com
Crystals, healing jewelry, psychic readings. Welcoming vibe, good energy. Great for readings if the Jackson Square readers are busy.
Boutique du Vampyre
709½ St. Ann St · feelthebite.com
World-class vampire boutique. Fortune candles (pewter charms inside), handmade jewelry, capes, curiosities. Also your gateway to the Potions Lounge speakeasy hidden inside.
Jackson Square Tarot Readers
In front of St. Louis Cathedral · Cash
The readers set up daily along the fence. Quality varies — if one doesn't feel right, walk to the next. $20–40. Genevieve Fynnyx is consistently recommended if she's there.
Clothing & Boutiques
Royal Street (French Quarter) and Magazine Street (Uptown) are the two best shopping corridors.
Trashy Diva
Royal St (French Quarter) + Magazine St locations
NOLA's most iconic women's boutique. Retro-inspired pieces, dramatic silhouettes, perfect for the group's aesthetic.
Hemline
Magazine Street
Curated women's clothing and accessories. Trendy but wearable. Local favorite with solid seasonal stock.
Monomin
Lower Magazine St
Elevated neutrals — Third Form, AG Jeans, Grown Alchemist. Quieter vibe, quality-focused. Worth it if you're on Magazine anyway.
Joseph
Magazine Street

Bourbon Street — Yes, There Are Actually Shops
Worth a daylight stroll even if nighttime Bourbon isn't your thing. These are the non-bar stops worth noting.
Bourbon French Parfums
Bourbon St · French Quarter
Custom fragrances and skincare inspired by New Orleans history. One of the oldest perfumeries in America. Worth 20 minutes regardless of whether you buy anything.
Goorin Bros. Hat Shop
Bourbon St · French Quarter
Excellent hat selection — fedoras, vintage styles, very wearable. If you've been looking for a great hat, this is the place.
KREWE Eyewear
Bourbon St · French Quarter
Local NOLA brand. Genuinely stylish, unlike most tourist-area eyewear shops. Great for sunglasses.
Southern Candymakers
Bourbon St · French Quarter
Old-school pralines, chocolates, confections made on-site. Watch them make pralines through the window. Good gift option if you're bringing anything home.
Note on Bourbon Street timing: Daylight only for shopping. The vibe shifts around 6pm. If you want to pop into any of these shops, do it mid-afternoon, not at night.

Getting Around
Transportation in New Orleans
Rideshare
Uber and Lyft both work well in NOLA. French Quarter → MSY: ~$25–35, 25 min. French Quarter → JAMNOLA: ~$7, 5 min. Keep the app ready — surge can hit weekend nights on Bourbon Street.
St. Charles Streetcar
$1.25/ride or $3/day Jazzy Pass. Runs from Canal St through the CBD and Garden District to Uptown. Washington Ave stop = Commander's Palace. Takes 20–30 min end to end.
The French Quarter on Foot
Almost everything in your itinerary is walkable from The Roosevelt. The hotel is at the edge of the FQ at the CBD border — most venues are 5–20 min on foot.
JAMNOLA (Frenchmen St)
~5-min Uber from the French Quarter. 10-min walk if you feel like it. The Marigny is the neighborhood east of the Quarter — worth exploring around Frenchmen St for live music and local bars while you're there.
Magazine Street
~15-min Uber from the French Quarter OR take the St. Charles streetcar toward Uptown and get off near Magazine. Street runs parallel to the river in the Garden District/Uptown — 6 miles of boutiques.
Airport (MSY)
Louis Armstrong New Orleans International. ~25–30 min from French Quarter by rideshare. For the 6:20am Monday departure, allow 25 min and add buffer — pre-schedule Uber for 4:15am the night before.
